Shibuya

The famous crossing, teen culture & everything beyond.

Explore stops ↓ Book a tour
Daikanyama · Harajuku · Nakameguro · Shibuya

Shibuya is where Tokyo's present tense happens. The scramble crossing — five streams of people converging at once from every direction — has become the defining image of the modern city, but it's only the beginning. Behind it lies a district of sharp contrasts: teenage fashion temples and serious art museums, canal-side coffee shops and thundering club nights, the world's most famous dog statue and one of the most beautifully designed train stations on earth. Spend a full day here and you'll understand why half the world's idea of Tokyo was formed in this single postcode.

Daikanyama

代官山

A ten-minute walk west from Shibuya, Daikanyama is the city's most quietly fashionable neighbourhood — tree-lined streets of independent boutiques, architect-designed apartment blocks, and the extraordinary Tsutaya Books T-Site, a sprawling bookshop and lifestyle complex set in a low-rise wood-and-glass campus that has become one of the best-designed retail environments in Asia.

💡 Tsutaya T-Site is open until midnight and the Anjin bar inside is a particularly good spot for a mid-afternoon coffee or an evening drink.

Landmark · Hachiko Plaza

Hachiko Statue & Plaza

ハチ公像

Outside the west exit of Shibuya Station, a bronze Akita dog sits on a low plinth and has been Tokyo's most beloved meeting point since the statue was unveiled in 1934.

💡 Visit early morning before the crowds arrive for a clearer view and easier photos.
