Yanaka

Old shitamachi — the Tokyo that time forgot. Winding pathways, preserved wooden temples, and local artisan legacy.

Explore highlights ↓ Check availability
Wartime Preservations · Neighborhood Alleys · Community Soul

Yanaka escaped wartime destruction and remains one of Tokyo's most authentic historical quarters. Its narrow back alleys, historic residential boundaries, and sprawling local craft legacy showcase a vibrant community charm that preserves the historic heart of old downtown Tokyo.

Best For Slow travel, photography, authentic traditional atmosphere, and localized fine arts
Best Time to Visit Mid-morning on a weekday — exceptionally quiet, unhurried, and blessed with perfect soft light

Historic Grounds · Natural Sanctuaries

Yanaka Cemetery

谷中霊園
📍 View on Google Maps ↗

Stroll along beautiful, deeply atmospheric paths where Tokyo’s elite and historical figures rest. This open park-like expanse features beautiful old tomb structures and exceptional natural beauty that adapts beautifully across each passing season.

💡 This sanctuary offers stunning cherry blossom canopies in the spring and peaceful firefly-lit evenings during late summer.

Stop 02 · Local Charm — Late Morning · 11:15 AM
Alleyway Icons · Neighborhood Wildlife

Cat Town Corners

猫の町
📍 View on Google Maps ↗

Yanaka is legendary across the capital for its thriving, cherished community of resident street cats. Keep your camera ready as you walk, as you will spot them lounging lazily across weathered wooden fencing, tiled roofs, and sunny doorsteps.

💡 Local shops have completely embraced this identity, selling beautiful handcrafted cat-themed pottery, souvenirs, and pastries.
★★★★★ 4.5 · 920+ reviews
Stop 03 · Yanaka Ginza — Mid-Day · 12:30 PM
Community Markets · Nostalgic Flavors

Yanaka Ginza

谷中銀座
📍 View on Google Maps ↗

Step down the famous Yuyake Dandan (Sunset Steps) into a charming, retro-style market street. This vibrant neighborhood strip is bursting with independent craft vendors, old-school clothing boutiques, and legendary local street snack stalls.

💡 Stand at the top of the steps late in the afternoon to catch one of the most celebrated urban sunset views in traditional Tokyo.
★★★★★ 4.2 · 4,600+ reviews
Stop 04 · Spiritual Pockets — Early Afternoon · 02:00 PM
Sacred Spaces · Edo Period Structures

Temple-Hopping Lanes

寺院巡り
📍 View on Google Maps ↗

Discover dozens of beautiful, historic temples quietly tucked behind ordinary residential corners. Because this district was spared from modern developers, these centuries-old wooden structures maintain an incredibly raw, understated architectural intimacy.

💡 Look out for the historic, unique mud-and-tile walls (Tsujibei) that still border several of the old temple boundaries.
★★★★★ 4.5 · 1,200+ reviews
Stop 05 · Creative Hubs — Late Afternoon · 03:30 PM
Artisan Craft · Contemporary Adaptations

Art Gallery District

ギャラリー街
📍 View on Google Maps ↗

Experience an inspiring architectural renaissance where historical structures find modern purpose. Dozens of independent contemporary art galleries, designer ateliers, and independent pottery studios are set inside beautifully restored traditional townhouses and old public bathhouses.

💡 Make time to stop by Scai the Bathhouse, a widely celebrated contemporary gallery located inside an old 200-year-old public bath structure.
★★★★★ 4.6 · 340+ reviews
