Hakone Day Trip

Mountain railways, volcanic valley views, and a cruise across Lake Ashi beneath Mt. Fuji.

Explore highlights ↓ Check availability
Volcanic Calderas · Switchback Rail · Sacred Horizons

Hakone is a vast, beautiful national park region nestled in the volcanic mountains near Mt. Fuji. Traveling here is half the fun — the classic "Hakone Loop" involves an old switchback mountain train, a steep cable car, a ropeway over smoking sulphur vents, and a cruise across a crater lake on a pirate boat. Terry handles all the logistics, ticketing, and timing, so you can just look at the mountains.

Duration Full day (~8-10 hrs)
Group Size Solo & Small Private Groups Only
Languages English & Fluent Japanese
Our Path · The Hakone Loop
Stop 01 · Historic Transits · Alpine Engineering

Tozan Mountain Railway

箱根登山電車
📍 View on Google Maps ↗

Board Japan's oldest mountain railway as it climbs steeply through deep, densely forested valleys. Experience classic switchback techniques where the train alternates directions to conquer the extreme mountain grade.

💡 Gorgeous hydrangeas frame the tracks in June, turning into rich fiery foliage deep into autumn.
Stop 02 · Sulphur Springs · Geothermal Peaks

Owakudani Valley

大涌谷
📍 View on Google Maps ↗

Take the high-altitude ropeway over an active volcanic zone defined by rising steam vents, boiling hot springs, and sweeping mountain panoramas. Taste the famous Kuro-tamago — local eggs boiled directly in sulfur-rich waters until their shells turn ink black.

💡 Local lore says eating just one of these eggs adds seven years to your life expectancy.
Stop 03 · Scenic Sails · Shoreline Shrines

Lake Ashi Cruise

芦ノ湖
📍 View on Google Maps ↗

Sail across a beautifully clear crater lake formed thousands of years ago. From the open-air deck, observe the iconic bright red torii gate of Hakone Shrine rising dramatically directly out of the deep blue water.

💡 On pristine days, the surface of the lake creates a stunning mirror reflection of Mount Fuji.
Stop 04 · Tokaido Highway · Heritage Groves

Cedar Avenue Walk

杉並木
📍 View on Google Maps ↗

Walk down a perfectly preserved dirt section of the old historic Tokaido highway. Trek beneath towering, 400-year-old giant cedar trees planted long ago to shade travelers marching between Kyoto and old Edo.

💡 This atmospheric trail offers a quiet, meditative escape directly from modern tour crowds.
