Shinjuku

Neon, nightlife, and the electric pulse of the world's busiest train station.

Explore highlights ↓ Check availability
Gleaming Skylines · Historic Alleys · Cultural Junctions

Shinjuku is Tokyo at full volume — a district of extreme contrasts where gleaming skyscrapers share streets with the narrow red-lit lanes of Golden Gai and Kabukicho. It's exhausting, thrilling, and completely unmissable. Terry knows every corner, from the tiny jazz bars that hold ten people to the rooftop views that make the whole city snap into focus.

Best For Nightlife, photography, people-watching & urban energy
Best Time to Visit Late afternoon into evening (neon looks best after dark)

Entertainment District
Neon Streets · Urban Nightlife

Kabukicho

歌舞伎町

Dive headfirst into Tokyo's most famous entertainment quadrant. Walk through towering glowing archways into a sensory landscape of massive digital display boards, bustling game centers, and dynamic multi-story architecture that truly comes alive after sundown.

💡 Look skyward between the alley buildings to spot the famous giant Godzilla head looming over the cinema complex.

02
Historic Alleys
Micro-Bars · Retro Architecture

Golden Gai

新宿ゴールデン街

Explore a dense, low-slung maze of six architectural alleyways frozen beautifully in Tokyo's post-war era. This legendary enclave packs over 200 tiny, deeply atmospheric second-story micro-bars, each featuring its own unique subculture style, musical niche, and legacy flavor.

💡 Many doors hide steep stairways to ten-seat rooms; Terry knows exactly which hidden corners are warmly welcoming to international travelers.
Stop 03
03
Imperial Gardens
Botanical Escapes · Landscape Design

Shinjuku Gyoen

新宿御苑

Experience an oasis that feels beautifully impossible amid the surrounding neon grid. This expansive imperial garden seamlessly transitions between formal French geometric landscapes, English landscape expansive lawns, and traditional historic Japanese ponds.

💡 The frames here are spectacular, capturing pristine traditional reflections with towering skyscrapers framing the sky above.
Stop 04
04
Retail Monoliths
Premier Shopping · High-End Depachika

Takashimaya Times Square

タカシマヤタイムズスクエア

Witness modern retail engineered on an incredible scale. Beyond the world-class shopping floors, explore the legendary basement 'depachika' food halls—a premium underground culinary paradise showcasing artisanal sweets, bento craft, and seasonal delicacies.

💡 The complex features open-air upper terrace walkways that offer unique bird's-eye perspectives over the complex train tracks below.

05
Sacred Spaces
Shinto Sanctuaries · Quiet Enclaves

Hanazono Shrine

花園神社

Step instantly out of the urban roar into a historic Shinto sanctuary tucked quietly behind Shinjuku's main thoroughfares. Dedicated to fertility, performance arts, and commercial success, its bright vermilion structures have guarded the district's creative soul since the Edo period.

💡 Walking through its peaceful courtyard path provides a wonderful palette cleanser before navigating back out to the neon avenues.
