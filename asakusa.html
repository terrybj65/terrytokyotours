Asakusa & Kappabashi

Witness the roots of early Tokyo at Senso-ji temple, before diving into the absolute mecca of artisan kitchen tools and handmade culinary treasures.

Explore highlights ↓ Check availability
Edo Heritage · Kitchen Masterpieces · Historic Enclaves

This dynamic route bridges the ancient spiritual past with contemporary artisan mastery. Terry guides you down the dramatic entryways of Senso-ji—Tokyo's oldest temple—through the vibrant retail backstreets of historic Asakusa, and directly into the wholesale blocks of Kappabashi, where top international chefs source handmade knives and culinary items.

Best For Traditional religious architecture, historic backstreets, artisan kitchen knives, and authentic culinary shopping
Best Time to Visit Morning to late afternoon — ideal for catching the historic shops active and getting private access to knife makers
Our Path · Temple & Artisan Corridors
Stop 01 · Spiritual Gateway — Morning · 09:30 AM
Tokyo's Oldest Temple · Market Path

Senso-ji & Nakamise

浅草寺 · 仲見世通り

Walk beneath the massive red paper lantern of the Kaminarimon (Thunder Gate) and down Nakamise-dori's centuries-old retail stalls. Terry navigates the grand main hall and spectacular five-story pagoda, revealing ancient layout details missed by standard tourist crowds.

💡 Terry steers you off the main paths to taste fresh, authentic family-made rice cakes made in the neighborhood for decades.
Stop 02 · Artisan Mecca — Afternoon · 01:30 PM
Wholesale Tool District · Handmade Knives

Kappabashi Kitchen Town

合羽橋道具街

Explore the legendary district serving Tokyo’s professional culinary trade for over a century. Browse hand-forged Japanese chef knives, beautifully glazed regional ceramics, historic lacquerware, and the famous, incredibly realistic food wax models.

💡 Terry provides translation help directly at exclusive knife studios, ensuring you find a blade shaped perfectly for your home kitchen needs.
