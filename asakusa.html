Asakusa

Old Tokyo — historic temple courtyards, traditional rickshaws, the fragrance of burning incense, and artisan pathways.

Edo Legacies · Sacred Spaces · Artisan Kitchen Alleys

Asakusa is the part of Tokyo that survived — or at least remembers — the Edo period. Senso-ji temple draws millions, but step one street back from the tourist flow and you're in a neighbourhood of traditional craft shops, sembei bakeries, and residents who've lived here for generations. Terry loves this area in the early morning, before the tour groups arrive.

Best For Culture, historic temples, traditional artisan crafts & photography
Best Time to Visit Early morning (7–9 AM) to avoid crowds, or late afternoon for golden light

Sacred Heart
Ancient Shrines · Buddhist Architecture

Senso-ji Temple

浅草寺

Explore Tokyo's oldest and most historically significant temple landmark. Pass through the monumental Kaminarimon (Thunder Gate) to witness majestic five-story pagodas, massive red paper lanterns, and deep spiritual traditions unchanged for centuries.

💡 Arriving in the early morning allows you to admire the tranquil temple architecture peacefully, completely free of the mid-day tourist rush.
Stop 02
02
Historic Market
Edo Marketplaces · Street Food Craft

Nakamise-dori

仲見世通り

Stroll along a vibrant approach market leading directly up to the temple sanctuary. Lined with century-old family stalls, it is the perfect spot to sample freshly prepared regional street snacks like sweet Ningyo-yaki, crispy melonpan, and warm hand-grilled rice crackers.

💡 Look closely at the shop shutters if you're early; they feature beautiful painted murals depicting historic seasonal festivals.

Artisan Capital
Culinary Craft · Culinary Tools

Kappabashi Kitchen Town

合羽橋道具街

Discover Tokyo’s wholesale culinary paradise, tucked neatly between Asakusa and Ueno. This famous mercantile street caters directly to chefs and home cooks alike, showcasing exquisite hand-forged Japanese kitchen knives, lacquerware, and hyper-realistic wax food replicas (sampuru).

💡 This is the ultimate neighborhood to pick up an incredibly sharp, custom-engraved kitchen knife or unique culinary souvenirs to bring home.

04
Vintage Transport
Historic Transit · Hidden Backalleys

Traditional Rickshaws

人力車

Immerse yourself completely in the historic character of the district. Pulled by deeply knowledgeable local guides, a rickshaw journey weaves seamlessly through hidden, narrow residential alleyways and architectural corridors that standard tour buses simply can't access.

💡 Rickshaws provide an excellent, comfortable vantage point for capturing beautiful, unique low-angle photographs of Old Tokyo.

Waterfront Views
Riverside Parks · Skyline Panoramas

Sumida River

隅田川

Conclude your exploratory walk along the sweeping, scenic banks of the Sumida River. Famous as Tokyo's premier capital for cherry blossom viewing during spring, the promenade offers beautiful paths framing modern architectural designs across the river waters.

💡 From the riverbanks, you can capture spectacular, uninhibited frames matching historic waters with the towering Tokyo Skytree.
